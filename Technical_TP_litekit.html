Lite Kit - Technical Aspect

Iterative Development Approach


In this project, the iterative development approach was critical for refining the system's functionality. This method involved repeated cycles of testing, analysis, and improvement, ensuring the robotic system could effectively track and follow the target object.

The first phase focused on sensor testing to ensure accurate detection. This involved validating the PixyCam's ability to identify the specific color and signature under various conditions.

In the second phase, the Lite kit was tested for movement towards a box upon detecting the target color. This ensured the system could respond correctly to the detection data provided by the PixyCam.

The final phase involved full-scale color tracking, where the robot demonstrated its capability to follow the target object dynamically. This phase showcased the integration of the PixyCam, STM32 microcontroller, and Parallax Propeller Board working together seamlessly.

Hardware Communication Protocols


In this project, understanding and implementing hardware communication protocols were critical for ensuring reliable data transmission and system functionality. A key aspect was learning about checksums, which play a crucial role in maintaining data integrity during transmission.

The first phase involved developing a method to transmit PixyCam2 data to the STM32 board. This required creating a robust color tracking algorithm capable of processing data to determine the color's position and distance. Ensuring accurate and error-free data transmission was achieved by implementing checksums to verify the integrity of the transmitted data.

The second phase focused on integrating the PixyCam2 with the STM32 and transmitting the processed data to the Parallax Propeller Board using the USART communication protocol. This involved developing a tracking algorithm on the STM32, which used the color position data to determine the direction and distance for movement.

Finally, the Parallax Propeller Board used the data received via USART to control the movement direction based on the STM32's algorithm. This integration showcased the seamless communication between the PixyCam2, STM32 microcontroller, and Parallax Propeller Board, demonstrating effective hardware communication protocols and the importance of data integrity checks.
